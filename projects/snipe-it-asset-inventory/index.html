Work Project

IT Asset Inventory and Site Tracking System using Snipe-IT

This project focused on improving how IT equipment was tracked across multiple sites by building a more structured asset inventory using Snipe-IT.

The goal was to make equipment easier to locate, assign, audit, return, replace, and manage throughout its lifecycle. The system organised assets, accessories, users, desk locations, site maps, naming standards, and lifecycle statuses into a clearer operational structure.

Privacy note: Real company names, user names, serial numbers, asset tags, desk numbers, locations, screenshots, and internal references have been removed or recreated in a sanitised format.

Problem

Why This Project Was Needed

Without a reliable asset inventory structure, IT equipment can become difficult to track. Devices may be assigned to users without clear records, accessories can be overlooked, desk locations can become outdated, and available stock can be harder to confirm quickly.

Before

  • Device locations were harder to confirm quickly.
  • Asset records were not always structured consistently.
  • Accessories were harder to track alongside main devices.
  • Returned or spare equipment needed clearer lifecycle status.
  • Desk and site information needed better visibility.

Target State

  • Clear asset ownership and assignment records.
  • Better visibility of available and assigned equipment.
  • Consistent naming and asset tag structure.
  • Improved desk, site, and location tracking.
  • More reliable information for audits and planning.

Approach

Inventory Design Approach

The project was designed around creating a practical structure that could be searched, filtered, updated, and maintained over time. The focus was not only adding assets into Snipe-IT, but making the records useful for real IT work.

Inventory Flow

Asset Record • User Assignment • Location • Lifecycle Status

Device or accessory
        ↓
Snipe-IT asset record
        ↓
Assigned user or available stock
        ↓
Site, area, and desk location
        ↓
Lifecycle status
        ↓
Audit, support, onboarding, offboarding, or replacement decision

This made Snipe-IT a central reference point for locating equipment, checking who was using it, confirming where it was placed, and understanding its current status.

Operational Use

How The System Supported IT Workflows

Onboarding: prepared equipment could be assigned to new starters with a clearer record of device, accessories, and desk location.

Offboarding: assigned equipment could be checked against the user record so laptops, monitors, docks, chargers, and accessories could be returned.

Troubleshooting: device ownership and location could be confirmed faster when investigating hardware, access, or endpoint issues.

Audits: asset records could be reviewed against physical equipment, assigned users, site locations, and lifecycle status.

Hardware planning: available, assigned, returned, and retired equipment could be reviewed more clearly when planning replacements or new setups.

Quality Control

Checks and Record Validation

After the inventory structure was improved, records were checked for accuracy, consistency, and usefulness.

  • Checked asset records for missing information.
  • Reviewed naming consistency across devices and accessories.
  • Checked assigned users against known equipment records.
  • Reviewed location and desk information.
  • Checked accessory quantities and availability.
  • Reviewed lifecycle statuses for returned, spare, or retired equipment.
  • Updated records where inconsistencies were found.
